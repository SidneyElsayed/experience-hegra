What's Included

✓ Free parking at Winter Park or the Hegra Visitor Center

✓ Welcome and hospitality (seasonal drinks, dates, sweets) at the Visitor Center

✓ Transportation within Hegra by bicycle or horse-drawn carriage

✓ Entrance to various designated heritage sites within Hegra

✓ English & Arabic speaking Rawis (storytellers) and Rangers (heritage support) on-site to offer historical context and guidance

✕ Transportation to and from the Hegra Visitor Center

✕ Entry inside tombs; only exterior viewing is permitted during the tour

Meeting Location

Hegra Visitor Center

If you'd like, you can drive directly to Hegra for the start of the tour. Make sure to use the Hegra Visitor Center Google pin, as others won't take you directly to the entrance. Free parking is available behind the Visitor Center.

View Google pin for the Hegra Visitor Center

What to Expect

Saudi is well known for its hospitality, which they are excited to share. From the moment you step into the Visitor Center, you'll be welcomed with an array of drinks and treats. Depending on the season, this could be fresh juices or Saudi coffee, water, dried fruits and always a few dates. Most of the tour guides, known as Rawis (singular: raw-wee), are from AlUla or the surrounding area. Each guide is accompanied by a Ranger, who is there to ensure visitors respect the heritage sites.

Your Rawi will through the area of Hegra, bringing you to various designated sites either by carriage or by bicycle. At each stop, they will explain different details of the tombs, carvings and more. The tour generally includes the following:

  • Jabal Ahmar
  • Tomb of Lihyan son of Kuza

*Some sites may be under renovation/restoration at the time of your visit - parts of Hegra are still being discovered, which is what makes it an exciting time to visit.
